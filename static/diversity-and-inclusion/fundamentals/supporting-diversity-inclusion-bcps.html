"5 Things We Learned About Creating a Successful Workplace Diversity Program" - Harvard Business Review

"5 Things We Learned About Creating a Successful Workplace Diversity Program" - Harvard Business Review

Note: the Harvard Business Review offers two articles for free each month for guest users. If you register your email with the site, it will offer 4 free articles per month. If you have already reached your maximum of free articles this month, you can bookmark this resource to come back to later when your free articles renew next month!"Research has found that most workplace diversity programs fail to produce meaningful diversity and inclusion, and some have actually increased bias among individual employees." So what to do? One large, federally funded institute reviews what they learned in creating a successful workplace diversity program. Their five key practices for how to implement a successful workplace diversity program can inform our BCPS D&I strategy as well:
  1. Focus on intervention, not just bias reduction;
  2. Invite non-managers to foster communication across the organization;
  3. Keep the focus on workplace issues, not personal ones;
  4. Keep the conversation going to stay accountable; and
  5. Be flexible, in both content and delivery.

Three Myths about Allyship – and what Research Says Instead.

Three Myths about Allyship – and what Research Says Instead.

As the interest in allyship swells, so does the risk for misinterpretation. This article dispels the following myths:
  • Myth 1: Awareness is Enough
    Reality: Awareness must be coupled with action.
  • Myth 2: Allyship is Friendship
    Reality: Friendship is about creating connection. Allyship is about elevating equality.
  • Myth 3: Once an Ally, Always an Ally
    Reality: Allyship is built, not attained.

Policy Essentials in the BC Public Service

This short course provides BC public servants with an introduction to the topic of public policy. The course will provide you with a basic understanding of public policy, the phases of policy development, and the attributes and skills to be successful in a policy role. By the end of this course, you can expect to be able to: - define public policy. - recognize factors that influence the public policy environment. - recognize what policy analysis is and the role of policy analysts. - identify phases of the policy development cycle. - recognize the skills and attributes to be an effective policy analyst.
